# Stay Safe Online: Digital Safety Checklist

Here are 8 actionable tips to protect your digital identity and finances:

The "No PIN for Receiving" Rule: Always remember that you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money; it is only required for sending or authorizing a payment.

Verify UPI 'Collect Requests': If a stranger sends you a "Collect Request" on a UPI app claiming it is for a prize or a refund, decline it immediately to prevent unauthorized deductions.

If you fall victim to financial fraud, report it within the first two hours (the "Golden Hour") to increase the chances of the bank freezing the stolen funds.

Activate 2FA on your university portal, email, and social media to ensure a hacker cannot log in even if they steal your password.

Regularly check your phone settings and revoke access to your camera, microphone, or gallery for apps you no longer use.

Before clicking "urgent" internship or job links, manually check the sender's email address for slight misspellings or anomalies.

When using computers in university labs or libraries, always use "Incognito" mode and manually log out of all sessions to protect your data from the next user.

Use clear subject lines and formal language in academic emails to avoid your requests being flagged as spam or appearing unprofessional.
