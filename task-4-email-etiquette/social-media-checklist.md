## Do’s
1.Audit your privacy settings every semester to ensure personal posts aren't accidentally visible to recruiters or the public.

2.Maintain a professional profile on sites like LinkedIn, using a clear headshot and a bio that highlights your skills.

3.Fact-check before you share viral news or sensitive information to avoid spreading "fake news" or misinformation.

4.Showcase your achievements by posting about your projects, internships, or volunteer work to build a positive "digital brand."

5.Ask before you tag friends or batchmates in photos, respecting their right to control their own digital footprint.

## Don’ts
1.Don't post in the heat of the moment. Avoid venting about professors, classes, or employers while you are angry or frustrated.

2.Don't share sensitive data like your home address, phone number, student ID, or live location in real-time.

3.Don't engage with "trolls" or online bullies, as these interactions can be screenshotted and used against you later.

4.Don't post "Red Flag" content, such as photos involving illegal activities or violations of your university’s code of conduct.

5.Don't use weak passwords. Avoid using your name or birthdate; use unique passwords and enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).
